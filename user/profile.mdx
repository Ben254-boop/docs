Your profile page in the JKUAT Inventory Management System lets you update your personal details and change the password that was assigned to you by the administrator. Keeping your profile up to date ensures that your information is accurate and your account is secure.

## Accessing Your Profile

Click your name or the **Profile** link in the navigation menu. Your profile page shows your current account details.

## Updating Personal Information

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Profile Settings">
    On the Profile page, click **Edit Profile** or the edit icon next to your details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update Your Details">
    You can update the following fields:

    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | Full Name | Your display name in the system |
    | Email Address | Your institutional email address |
    | Department | Contact your administrator to change your department assignment |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click **Save Changes**. Your updated information is reflected immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your username and role (User/Admin) cannot be changed from the Profile page. Contact your system administrator if these need to be updated.
</Note>

## Changing Your Password

For security, change your password from the administrator-assigned default as soon as you first log in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Change Password">
    On the Profile page, scroll down to the **Change Password** section or click the **Change Password** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Current and New Password">
    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Current Password | Your existing password |
    | New Password | Your chosen new password (minimum 8 characters) |
    | Confirm New Password | Re-enter the new password to confirm |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Update Password**. You will be logged out and redirected to the login page. Log in with your new password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If you forget your password, you cannot reset it yourself. Contact your JKUAT system administrator to have your password reset.
</Warning>

## Password Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use a Strong Password" icon="shield">
    Use at least 8 characters with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep It Private" icon="lock">
    Never share your password with colleagues or write it down in an accessible place.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
